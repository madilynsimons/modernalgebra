\item Let $I$ be a nonempty ideal in $F$ such that $I$ does not equal $F$.
Let $x \in F$ and $x \notin I$, let $a \in I$, and let $a^{-1}$ be the

$a^{-1}ax \in I$.  However, $a^{-1}ax = 1_{F}x = x$ and $x \notin I$ so this
is a contradiction.
